How puberty changes your brain

00:06

While we often talk about puberty’s effect on the body, what gets overlooked are the fascinating changes that happen in the brain.

虽然我们经常谈论青春期对身体的影响，但被忽视的是大脑中发生的迷人变化.

00:29

I’ve been sent here to talk to you about puberty. Any questions?

我被派来这里是为了和你谈谈青春期。有什么问题吗？

00:37

During puberty, your reproductive organs grow and mature. Any questions?

在青春期，你的生殖器官会发育成熟。有什么问题吗？

00:45

This ripening allows you to become a sexually mature adult.

这种成熟让你成为性成熟的成年人.

00:55

There are so many other changes to your body that puberty can seem almost magical.

你的身体还有很多其他变化，青春期看起来几乎是神奇的.

01:04

I think I’ll take it from here.

我想我会从这里开始.

01:06

Puberty, in fact, begins in the brain.
At some point, usually between the ages of 9 and 14, puberty is triggered when a region known as the hypothalamus releases waves of a specialized hormone.

事实上，青春期始于大脑.
在某一时刻，通常在9岁到14岁之间，当一个被称为下丘脑的区域释放一种特殊激素波时，青春期就会触发.

01:19

As convenient as it would be to go to sleep a child and wake up an adult, this maturation is slow, and puberty lasts as long as 4 to 5 years.
And during this extended process, the brain undergoes its own transformation, thanks to two of puberty’s key players— estrogen and testosterone.

尽管孩子睡觉和成人醒来都很方便，但这种成熟是缓慢的，青春期长达4到5年.
在这个漫长的过程中，大脑经历了自己的转变，这要归功于青春期的两个关键角色——雌激素和睾酮.

01:39

Produced in the developing testes and ovaries, these hormones hitch a ride to the brain via the bloodstream.
Once there, they interact with receptors on neurons, changing the way the individual cells work and function by making them more or less excitable, altering their growth, or reshaping their connections.
Cumulatively, this can change how you feel, think, and behave.

这些激素产生于发育中的睾丸和卵巢，通过血流进入大脑.
一旦到达那里，它们就会与神经元上的受体相互作用，通过使单个细胞或多或少易兴奋、改变其生长或重塑其连接，从而改变单个细胞的工作和功能.
累积起来，这会改变你的感受、思考和行为.

02:01

For example, hormones remodel and develop the limbic system, a collection of brain regions responsible for emotional behavior.
Research in animal models suggests that the amygdala undergoes changes in size and connectivity during puberty.
The amygdala’s function is wide-ranging, from detecting threats in your environment, to helping you recognize emotions in your friend’s faces.
Its development allows you to better connect with your peers, while priming your brain for learning and discovery.

例如，荷尔蒙重塑和发展边缘系统，这是负责情绪行为的大脑区域的集合.
动物模型研究表明，青春期期间，杏仁核的大小和连接性发生了变化.
杏仁核的功能非常广泛，从检测环境中的威胁，到帮助你识别朋友脸上的情绪.
它的发展使你能够更好地与同龄人建立联系，同时为学习和发现大脑做好准备.

02:31

Likewise, puberty organizes and restructures the nucleus accumbens involved in reward and sensation-seeking.
Activity in this dopamine hub is thought to drive the pleasurable sensations we feel when doing rewarding activities, like spending time with friends or having new experiences.
Several studies have found that as hormone levels increase through puberty, so does the response of the nucleus accumbens.
As a result, exploration and social engagement may feel that much more important during adolescence.

同样，青春期会组织和重组参与奖赏和感觉寻求的伏隔核.
多巴胺中枢中的活动被认为会驱动我们在进行有益活动时所感受到的愉悦感，比如与朋友共度时光或有新的体验.
几项研究发现，随着青春期激素水平的增加，伏隔核的反应也会增加.
因此，在青少年时期，探索和社会参与可能会显得更加重要.

03:03

As these emotion and reward centers rapidly develop, their connections with higher cortical brain regions tend to do so on an extended timeline.
These cortical regions, which help impose emotional regulation and impulse control, continue to grow well past puberty, into your 20s.
While teens are often unfairly stereotyped as rash or impulsive, research reveals a more complex story.
Teens are just as capable as adults of making thoughtful decisions when given the time and space.
It’s only during high stress or in the heat of the moment that teens may find it more difficult to manage emotions.
Further, this lengthy cortical development allows adolescent brains to remain adaptable as they learn and grow in new situations, form their identities, and build the skill sets needed for adulthood.

随着这些情绪和奖励中心的快速发展，它们与大脑皮层较高区域的联系往往会在延长的时间线上进行。
这些有助于情绪调节和冲动控制的皮质区域，在青春期过后一直长到20多岁。
尽管青少年经常被不公平地定型为鲁莽或冲动，但研究揭示了一个更复杂的故事。
青少年和成年人一样有能力在有时间和空间的情况下做出深思熟虑的决定。
只有在压力大的时候，或者在最热的时候，青少年才可能发现更难控制情绪。
此外，这种长时间的皮层发育使青少年大脑在新情况下学习和成长，形成他们的身份，并建立成年所需的技能集时保持适应性.

03:54

For all we know about the effects of puberty on the brain, there remain many unanswered questions.
What sets off the initial puberty signal in the brain? Why is the average onset of puberty shifting earlier?
And, while hormones may seem powerful, they may not be the full story.
Experiences you have during adolescence may be just as influential as hormones in shaping and maturing the developing brain.
So while all these physical and mental changes can make you feel as if puberty is in control, you have more power than you think.
The everyday choices you make, from learning new skills, to being a good friend, to setting boundaries, ultimately steer the path of who you are and will become. Any questions?

尽管我们知道青春期对大脑的影响，但仍有许多未解答的问题.
是什么在大脑中触发了最初的青春期信号？为什么青春期的平均开始时间会提前？
而且，虽然荷尔蒙看起来很强大，但它们可能不是全部.
你在青春期的经历可能和荷尔蒙一样影响着发育中的大脑的形成和成熟.
所以，尽管所有这些身体和心理的变化都会让你觉得青春期在控制之中，但你拥有比你想象的更强大的力量.
你每天所做的选择，从学习新技能，到成为一个好朋友，再到设定界限，最终会指引你成为谁，也会成为谁. 有什么问题吗？
